Simulating Radio Synchrotron Morphology, Spectra, and Polarization of Cosmic Ray Driven Galactic Winds

This study investigates the role of cosmic ray-driven winds in shaping galactic radio emission by comparing mock observations from a state-of-the-art simulation with real radio data of the edge-on galaxy NGC 4217. Using the AREPO code with advanced cosmic ray transport and a multiphase interstellar medium, we find strong agreement between simulated and observed intensity profiles, spectra, and magnetic field morphology. The results support the idea that cosmic rays can drive large-scale galactic outflows and reproduce key features seen in radio observations, including the characteristic X-shaped magnetic fields.

H.-H. Sandy Chiu et al. (2024)

Boosting Supermassive Black Hole Growth in the Early Universe by Fuzzy Dark Matter Solitons

This work explores how fuzzy dark matter (FDM) solitons could help explain the rapid formation of supermassive black holes (SMBHs) in the early Universe. Using hydrodynamic simulations, we show that FDM solitons with masses around 109 M☉ naturally form by redshift z ≈ 7 and can significantly enhance gas accretion onto black hole seeds. The additional gravitational potential from these solitons boosts the Bondi accretion rate by 2–4 orders of magnitude, offering a plausible solution to the SMBH timing problem.

H.-H. Sandy Chiu et al. (2025)
